Academic Tree

A doctoral lineage from Carl Friedrich Gauss through quantum theory to multiscale materials modeling — two mathematical paths that meet at Carl Runge.

Compiled from the Mathematics Genealogy Project and institutional records. Through Gauss’s advisors, the tree reaches further back to the Byzantine scholar Georgios Gemistos Plethon (c. 1355–1452).

Deepest documented root · 14th–15th century

Georgios Gemistos Plethon

c. 1355/1360 – 1452/1454

Byzantine philosopher · Mystras

Neoplatonism · teacher of Bessarion

Earliest reasonably documented root of this Gauss branch. The full advisor chain below follows the Mathematics Genealogy Project path through Hausen’s co-advisor Planer.

Modern trunk · late 18th century

Carl Friedrich Gauss

1777 – 1855

Ph.D. Universität Helmstedt, 1799 · Advisor: Johann Friedrich Pfaff

Göttingen · “Prince of Mathematicians”

Number theory, astronomy, geodesy, magnetism, and the normal distribution. Both branches of this tree descend from his advisees.

Wikipedia · MGP

Two paths from Gauss · merging at Runge

Weierstraß line

CG

Christoph Gudermann

1798 – 1852

Göttingen, 1841 · Advisor: Gauss

Münster

Special functions and hyperbolic geometry; mentor of Weierstraß at Münster.

Wikipedia

Karl Weierstraß

1815 – 1897

Advisor: Gudermann · co-advisor of Runge

Universität Berlin

Founder of modern analysis; rigorized calculus and complex function theory.

Wikipedia · MGP

Kummer line

Friedrich Wilhelm Bessel

1784 – 1846

Göttingen, 1810 · Advisor: Gauss

Königsberg Observatory

Astronomer and mathematician; Bessel functions; first precise stellar parallax.

Wikipedia · MGP

Heinrich F. Scherk

1798 – 1885

Berlin, 1823 · Advisor: Bessel

Halle · Kiel

Minimal surfaces (Scherk surfaces) and analysis; advisor of Kummer.

Wikipedia

Ernst Kummer

1810 – 1893

Halle, 1831 · Advisor: Scherk · co-advisor of Runge

Universität Berlin

Number theorist; ideal numbers and early algebraic number theory.

Wikipedia

Paths converge

Carl Runge

1856 – 1927

Ph.D. Universität Berlin, 1880 · Advisors: Weierstraß & Kummer

Later Göttingen · applied mathematics & spectroscopy

Namesake of the Runge–Kutta methods; where the Gauss–Weierstraß and Gauss–Kummer lines reunite.

Wikipedia · MGP

Max Born

1882 – 1970 · Nobel Prize in Physics, 1954

Ph.D. Göttingen, 1906 · Advisor: Carl Runge

Göttingen · Edinburgh

Architect of quantum mechanics; probabilistic interpretation of the wave function. Mentored a generation that remade physics.

Wikipedia · MGP

J. Robert Oppenheimer

1904 – 1967

Dr. rer. nat. Göttingen, 1927 · Advisor: Max Born

Berkeley · Los Alamos · Institute for Advanced Study

Theoretical physicist; Born–Oppenheimer approximation; scientific director of the Manhattan Project. Doctoral students include Leslie Foldy and Willis Lamb.

Wikipedia · MGP

Leslie L. Foldy

1919 – 2001

Ph.D. UC Berkeley, 1948 · Advisor: J. Robert Oppenheimer

Case Western Reserve University

Foldy–Wouthuysen transformation; multiple scattering of waves; Institute Professor at Case.

MGP · Physics Today · Academic Tree

RO

Richard Kent Osborn

1919 – 1987

Ph.D. Case Institute of Technology, 1951 · Advisor: Leslie Foldy

University of Michigan · Nuclear Engineering

Neutron transport and quantum theory; coauthor with Sidney Yip of The Foundations of Neutron Transport Theory.

MGP

Sidney Yip

b. 1936

Ph.D. University of Michigan, 1963 · Advisor: Richard K. Osborn

MIT Nuclear Science & Engineering · Materials Science

Pioneer of molecular dynamics and multiscale materials modeling; mentor to a wide school of computational materials scientists.

MIT NSE · MGP

Wei Cai

Ph.D. MIT, 2001 · Advisor: Sidney Yip

Stanford University · Mechanical Engineering

Dislocation dynamics, statistical mechanics of defects, and multiscale modeling of materials.

Stanford Profiles · Group · MGP

Hanfeng Zhai

Ph.D. Candidate · Stanford University, present

Advisor: Wei Cai

Mechanics and Computation · Mechanical Engineering

Computational mechanics of materials, scientific machine learning, and multiscale modeling — continuing a line that runs from classical analysis through quantum theory to defect physics.

The earliest reasonably documented scholar in Gauss’s branching genealogy is Georgios Gemistos Plethon (c. 1355/1360–1452/1454). Complete MGP path: Plethon → Bessarion → Argyropoulos → Reuchlin → Melanchthon → Caselius → Calixt → Quenstedt → Walther → Pasch → Planer → Hausen → Kästner → Pfaff → Gauss. Then: Gauss → (Gudermann → Weierstraß | Bessel → Scherk → Kummer) → Runge → Born → Oppenheimer → Foldy → Osborn → Yip → Cai → Zhai.
