About me

Hanfeng Zhai (翟晗锋) is currently an incoming Ph.D. student in Mechanical Engineering at Stanford University. He was born and raised in Beijing. He received his M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Cornell University, and his B.S. in Theoretical & Applied Mechanics from Shanghai University. He was awarded Outstanding Graduate of Shanghai, Outstanding Undergraduate Thesis (etc.) by graduation. He jumped into mechanics mainly inspired by Wei-zang Chien.

His key research interest is multiscale computational mechanics. He has conducted research using molecular dynamics, discrete element method, and finite element method in computational modeling of complex natural phenomena such as the collision of bubbles, fatigue, and fracture of biomaterials and bio-inspired composite, biofilm attachment on the substrate, graphene fracture, etc. He also contributes to applying data-driven methods to mechanical problems, e.g., modifies physics-informed neural networks for fluids and nonlinear dynamics, and automates Bayesian optimization to design materials. He is essentially interested in combining these techniques for materials and systems design optimization. He has three main research themes: (1) Multiscale Computational Modeling; (2) Scientific Machine Learning; and (3) Computational Optimization for Materials Design. For more details, please visit his research.

During his undergrads, he played Denfensive End & Linebacker at SHU Bombers Football Team from 2017 to 2019. And he has trained with Russell Wilson at RW3 Passing Academy in 2018 (Media coverage on AFInt). He has won the 1st and 3rd place at SHU bodybuilding contests. In his spare time, he loves watching movies and hit the gym, and he also love history, philosophy, and social science.
